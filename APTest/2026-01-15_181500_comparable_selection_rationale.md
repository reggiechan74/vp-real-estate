## Selection Methodology

### USPAP/CUSPAP Compliance Framework

Per USPAP Standards Rule 1-4(a) and CUSPAP Section 4.2.3:

> *"An appraiser must collect, verify, and analyze all information necessary for credible assignment results... When sufficient market data exists in the subject's immediate market area, preference should be given to comparables from that area."*

### Comparable Selection Hierarchy

Appraisers follow a strict hierarchy when selecting comparables:

1. **Same submarket** (preferred)
2. **Adjacent submarket** (acceptable with adjustment)
3. **Similar submarket elsewhere** (last resort)

**Rationale:** Location is the hardest characteristic to adjust for because it encompasses hundreds of micro-factors (highway access, visibility, tenant demand, municipal taxes, zoning flexibility, labour pool). Physical differences (size, age, clear height) are quantifiable and adjustable with market-derived rates.

## Comparables Excluded: Mississauga (21 Properties)

### Primary Exclusion Reason: Different Industrial Submarket

Mississauga and Etobicoke operate as **distinct industrial submarkets** with materially different characteristics:

| Factor | Etobicoke (Subject) | Mississauga (Excluded) |
|--------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Municipal Jurisdiction** | City of Toronto | City of Mississauga (Peel Region) |
| **Property Tax Rate** | Toronto mill rate | Peel Region mill rate (different) |
| **Market Position** | Established infill market | Airport logistics/distribution hub |
| **Tenant Profile** | Manufacturing, local distribution | Logistics, e-commerce, 3PL |
| **Building Vintage** | 1950s-1980s predominant | 1990s-2010s predominant |
| **Typical Clear Heights** | 16-24 ft | 28-36 ft (new construction) |
| **Vacancy Rate** | <2% (supply constrained) | 2-4% (new supply available) |
| **Development Activity** | Limited (infill only) | Active (greenfield + infill) |

### Price Differential Evidence

The systematic price difference between submarkets demonstrates they are not directly comparable:

| Submarket | Average $/SF | Range | Sample Size |
|-----------|--------------|-------|-------------|
| **Etobicoke** | $205/SF | $173-$256 | 7 sales |
| **Mississauga** | $185/SF | $163-$280 | 21 sales |
| **Differential** | ~10% premium | - | - |

The Etobicoke premium reflects:
- Scarcity value (no new supply)
- Proximity to downtown Toronto
- Established infrastructure
- Higher land values per acre

### Location Adjustment Problem

Using Mississauga comparables would require a **location adjustment** to account for submarket differences. However:

1. **No paired sales available** to isolate location impact
2. **Multiple confounding variables** (building age, size, clear height all differ systematically)
3. **Speculative adjustment** would violate USPAP requirement for market-derived support
4. **Sufficient Etobicoke data exists** (5 usable comparables)

## Alternative Analysis Consideration

If Etobicoke comparables were insufficient (<3 sales), the analysis would have:

1. **Expanded to Mississauga** with explicit location adjustment
2. **Derived location adjustment** from paired sales or regression analysis
3. **Disclosed limitation** per CUSPAP Rule 6.2.17 (non-market-derived adjustment)
4. **Widened confidence interval** to reflect additional uncertainty

However, with 5 usable Etobicoke comparables, expansion was unnecessary and would have introduced avoidable uncertainty.

---

## Conclusion

The comparable selection process prioritized **geographic consistency** over sample size. Using 5 Etobicoke sales rather than 30 mixed-submarket sales:

| Approach | Pros | Cons |
|----------|------|------|
| **5 Etobicoke (Selected)** | No location adjustment needed; same tax/zoning jurisdiction; market-derived adjustments | Smaller sample; wider adjustment ranges |
| **30 Mixed (Rejected)** | Larger sample; statistical significance | Requires speculative location adjustment; mixing incompatible submarkets |

The selected approach produces a **more reliable value conclusion** despite the smaller sample size because:

1. All adjustments are **market-derived** or **cost-supported**
2. No **speculative location adjustments** required
3. **Coefficient of Variation (10.2%)** indicates good consistency
4. **USPAP/CUSPAP compliant** methodology
