## Common Residential Tenancy Issues

### Eviction Challenges:

**Non-Payment Evictions:**
- Tenant can void N4 by paying within 14 days
- Tenant can void L1 eviction order by paying before Sheriff enforcement (s.74)
- LTB relief from eviction discretion (s.83) - considers circumstances
- Payment plans and conditional orders

**Relief from Eviction:**
- LTB discretion based on all circumstances (s.83)
- Common factors: length of tenancy, reason for arrears, tenant's payment plan
- Statistics show 60%+ relief granted on first N4 if tenant pays arrears
- Conditional orders: pay arrears + ongoing rent or eviction enforced

**Landlord's Own Use (N12):**
- Good faith requirement - must genuinely intend to occupy
- Compensation required (1 month rent)
- 60 days notice minimum
- Bad faith penalties: tenant can claim 1 year rent or moving costs
- Evidence required: affidavit, personal testimony, plans for occupancy

**Illegal Evictions:**
- Self-help evictions prohibited (lock-outs, utility shut-offs)
- Substantial penalties: injunction, damages, rent abatement
- Criminal charges possible for serious violations
- Tenant remedies via T2 application

### Rent Regulation:

**Annual Guideline Increases:**
- 2024: 2.5%
- 2025: TBD (announced annually by Ministry)
- 90-day written notice required (proper form)
- Only one increase per 12 months

**Above-Guideline Increases (AGI):**
- Require LTB approval via application
- Based on capital improvements or operating cost increases
- Tenant right to participate and challenge
- Limited to 3% above guideline per year (9% total max)

**Unlawful Rent Increases:**
- Tenant can apply for T1 rent reduction
- Excess rent refunded plus 12 months going forward
- Common violations: no notice, insufficient notice, increase during 12-month period
- Vacancy rent increases (no limit between tenants, but cannot use illegal tactics)

**New Buildings Exemption:**
- Buildings first occupied after November 15, 2018 exempt from rent control
- Applies between tenancies (initial tenant still gets guideline protection)
- Landlord can set any rent for new tenant
- Still subject to other RTA protections

### Maintenance Issues:

**Landlord's Duty to Maintain (s.20):**
- Good repair and fit for habitation
- Comply with health, safety, housing standards
- Maintain building, HVAC, plumbing, electrical
- Provide and maintain appliances included in lease

**Vital Services (s.21):**
- Heat, electricity, water, fuel (as per lease)
- Strict obligations - cannot interrupt
- Tenant remedies: T2 application, T5 application, rent abatement
- Emergency situations may justify temporary interruption

**Emergency Repairs (s.26):**
- Tenant can arrange repairs if landlord fails to respond
- Reasonable notice to landlord first
- Can deduct up to one month's rent
- Must provide receipts and proper documentation

**Tenant Remedies:**
- T6 application for rent abatement
- T2 application if maintenance failure constitutes harassment
- Repair orders from LTB
- Rent abatement (percentage reduction based on impact)

### Assignment & Subletting:

**Tenant's Right (s.95, s.97):**
- Can request landlord consent to assign or sublet
- Landlord cannot arbitrarily refuse
- If landlord unreasonably refuses, tenant can terminate with 30 days notice

**Landlord's Options (s.95(4), s.97(3)):**
- Consent to assignment/sublet
- Refuse consent (must be reasonable)
- Refuse assignment but increase rent or terminate lease
- Must respond within 7 days or consent is deemed

**Process:**
- Tenant provides written request with proposed assignee/subtenant information
- Landlord has 7 days to respond
- If no response, deemed consent
- If refused, tenant can apply to LTB or terminate with 30 days notice

**Subtenant Rights:**
- Subtenant has RTA protections against landlord
- Cannot be evicted without LTB order
- Has standing to participate in proceedings
- Limited rights against sublandlord (original tenant)
