# Roadmap: Linear Infrastructure & Property Acquisition Toolkit

**Domain Focus:** Transit corridors, utility transmission, pipeline right-of-way, partial interest acquisitions

**Background:** Transit agency property acquisition + utility transmission real estate management experience

---

## Overview

Extend the VP Real Estate toolkit to cover **linear infrastructure acquisition and management** - the specialized domain where utilities, transit agencies, and pipeline companies acquire partial interests in land rather than full fee simple ownership.

**Key Differences from Commercial Leasing:**
- Acquiring rights, not space (easements, not leases)
- Involuntary transactions (expropriation as backstop)
- Long-term or permanent interests (not 5-10 year terms)
- Public sector stakeholders (municipalities, Indigenous communities, regulators)
- Political and regulatory complexity (environmental assessments, public consultation)

### Calculators (5)

1. **Easement Valuation Calculator**
   - Inputs: Fee simple value, easement type (utility, transit, pipeline), width, restrictions
   - Methods: Percentage of fee (5-25%), before/after, income capitalization
   - Outputs: Permanent easement value, annual rent equivalent, discount factors

2. **Partial Taking Calculator (Before/After Method)**
   - Inputs: Property before (size, value, use), property after (remaining size, value, highest & best use)
   - Calculations: Market value before, market value after, severance damages, injurious affection
   - Outputs: Total compensation, breakdown by component, adjustment factors

3. **Expropriation Compensation Calculator**
   - Inputs: Property details, market value, disturbance damages, business relocation costs
   - Statutory framework: Ontario Expropriations Act, Alberta Expropriation Act, federal acts
   - Outputs: Total statutory compensation, breakdown, interest calculations

4. **Right-of-Way Calculator**
   - Inputs: Corridor specs (width, length, voltage/capacity), property details, land use restrictions
   - Analysis: ROW area, encumbrance impact on development, access restrictions
   - Outputs: Market value reduction, easement compensation, ongoing restrictions value

5. **Comparable Sales Analyzer (Land)**
   - Inputs: Subject property, comparable sales (3-10 comps)
   - Adjustments: Location, size, zoning, access, services, time, encumbrances
   - Outputs: Adjusted comparable values, reconciliation, market value estimate

## Domain-Specific Considerations

### Regulatory Frameworks
- **Ontario Expropriations Act** - Primary statutory framework for transit agencies, utilities, municipalities
- **Federal Expropriation Act** - For federal infrastructure projects
- **Environmental Assessment Act** - Consultation and approval requirements
- **Duty to Consult** - Indigenous consultation obligations

### Stakeholder Types
- **Farmers** - Agricultural land impacts, severance of productive land, access issues
- **Homeowners** - Residential proximity concerns, property value impacts, construction disruption
- **Businesses** - Business losses, relocation costs, access and visibility impacts
- **Municipalities** - Road access, utility coordination, planning approvals
- **Indigenous Communities** - Treaty rights, traditional land use, consultation protocols

### Valuation Challenges
- **Easement percentages** - Market data scarce, wide range (5-35% of fee simple)
- **Injurious affection** - Difficult to quantify, subjective impacts (noise, visual, proximity)
- **Specialty properties** - No comparables (quarries, aggregate lands, industrial sites)
- **Agricultural land** - Productive vs. speculative value, soil quality, drainage

### Negotiation Dynamics
- **Expropriation threat** - Changes power dynamic vs. commercial leasing
- **Public interest** - Infrastructure benefits vs. individual property rights
- **Political sensitivity** - Media attention, community opposition, election cycles
- **Timeline pressure** - Project delays cost millions, settlement incentives high

## Competitive Landscape

**Current tools in market:**
- **Appraisal software** (ARGUS, a la mode) - General purpose, not infrastructure-specific
- **Project management tools** (Primavera, MS Project) - Timeline tracking, not valuation
- **GIS platforms** (ArcGIS, QGIS) - Mapping, not compensation analysis
- **Excel models** - Bespoke, inconsistent, error-prone, non-auditable

**Opportunity:**
- **No integrated solution** for linear infrastructure acquisition (valuation + process + negotiation)
- **Public sector need** - Transit agencies, utilities, municipalities, pipeline companies
- **Regulatory compliance** - Statutory compensation calculations (expropriation acts)
- **Defensibility** - Audit trails for hearings and public scrutiny

**Differentiation:**
- End-to-end workflow (valuation → negotiation → briefing → settlement)
- Statutory compliance built-in (Expropriations Act calculations)
- Expert skills for domain-specific guidance (expropriation, easements, Indigenous consultation)
- Open source (no vendor lock-in, customizable for agency-specific needs)
